Discover Jim Corbett National Park

Welcome to Jim Corbett

Established in 1936 as India’s first national park, Jim Corbett National Park is a haven for wildlife lovers and adventure seekers. Located in the picturesque state of Uttarakhand, nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, the park spans over 520 square kilometers and is home to the majestic Bengal tiger, leopards, elephants, and over 600 species of birds. Its diverse ecosystems, from dense sal forests to sprawling grasslands and the serene Kosi River, make it a must-visit destination. Our expertly crafted packages and activities ensure an immersive experience, blending adventure, relaxation, and education about the park’s rich biodiversity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Jim Corbett?

The best time is from November to June, when the weather is pleasant, and wildlife sightings are frequent. The park is closed during the monsoon season (July to October).

Are the safaris safe for children?

Yes, safaris are safe for children above 5 years, provided they are accompanied by adults. Guides ensure safety and provide child-friendly insights.

What should I pack for the trip?

Pack comfortable clothing, sturdy shoes, binoculars, a camera, insect repellent, sunscreen, and a hat. Neutral-colored clothing is recommended for safaris.

Can I book activities separately?

Yes, all activities can be booked individually or as part of a package. Use the booking forms provided for each activity.

Are there any restrictions in the park?

Visitors must follow park rules, including no littering, no loud noises, and staying within designated areas. Smoking and alcohol are prohibited.

Is accommodation included in packages?

Yes, all packages include accommodation, ranging from eco-friendly tents to luxury resorts, depending on the package chosen.
